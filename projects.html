    Research Projects

    Think Like AI

    This demo is designed to help students understand how Large Language Models (LLMs) work. The software has evolved over time to cover various LLM concepts.

    SpotAI

    Introduces students to deepfakes through hands-on activities: judging real vs. manipulated media, experimenting with AI image editing and face swapping, and reflecting on ethical scenarios involving misinformation and harm. Developed and tested with middle schoolers (Summer 2025).

    PromptPeek

    Engages students in prompt construction and reverse prompting activities to explore AI limitations like negation failures (e.g., "no elephants"), gendered stereotypes, privacy implications, and reliability issues. Developed and tested with middle schoolers (Summer 2025).

    Bandit Explorer

    Interactive tool demystifying recommender systems by letting learners adjust exploration parameters and observe how platforms like YouTube balance exploring new content with exploiting user preferences. Developed in summer 2025 and tested with middle schoolers.

    Next Word Adventure

    Demonstrates how n-gram models predict the next word. User study (N=48) showed significant improvements in understanding the statistical nature of AI.

    AI Chef Trainer

    Introduces children to the importance of data in ML through a cyclical process of testing, contributing, and retraining.

    Ask Me Anything

    Topic-specific chatbot using GPT-3 (davinci-002). Tailored responses for students to test AI knowledge and trust.
